## Executive Summary

Open Workforce Platform is an open-source platform for solving complex workforce optimisation problems using modern constraint solving, artificial intelligence, and cloud-native engineering.

Rather than focusing on a single industry, the platform is designed to support workforce scheduling, routing, resource allocation, and operational planning across healthcare, utilities, logistics, manufacturing, aviation, field services, and beyond.

The project is developed completely in the open. Every architectural decision, research finding, benchmark, and engineering trade-off will be documented so the project serves both as production-quality software and as an educational resource.

## Mission

Build the world's best open engineering platform for workforce optimisation.

---

## Vision

Create an open platform that organisations can genuinely use to solve real operational problems while showcasing modern software engineering, optimisation techniques, artificial intelligence, and cloud architecture.

---

## Why This Project Exists

This project began as an evolution of a university dissertation focused on nurse rostering and patient allocation.

Over the last decade, advances in optimisation, artificial intelligence, cloud computing, mapping technologies, and software engineering have fundamentally changed what is possible.

Rather than recreating the original dissertation, this project reimagines it as a modern engineering platform capable of solving workforce optimisation problems across multiple industries.

The project also serves as a long-term engineering portfolio, demonstrating how complex software should be researched, designed, implemented, tested, documented, and maintained.

## Target Users

- Organisations with complex workforce scheduling requirements
- Software engineers interested in optimisation
- Researchers and students studying operational research
- Organisations looking for an open and extensible optimisation platform

---

## Guiding Principles

1. Solve real-world problems
2. Engineering before implementation
3. Explain every architectural decision
4. Benchmark every optimisation
5. Prefer simplicity over unnecessary complexity
6. Build for extensibility
7. Keep the platform open and transparent
8. Treat documentation as a first-class deliverable

---

## Success Criteria

Version 1 will be considered successful when it:

- Produces valid workforce schedules using modern constraint solving
- Demonstrates measurable optimisation against benchmark datasets
- Provides clear documentation explaining every major engineering decision
- Can be deployed as a modern cloud-native application
- Is suitable for use as a professional engineering portfolio

---

## Long-Term Vision

Open Workforce Platform will become a modular optimisation platform capable of supporting multiple industries while remaining open-source, educational, and production-ready.

The project should demonstrate not only what was built, but also how experienced engineers approach complex problem solving, architecture, and software design.
